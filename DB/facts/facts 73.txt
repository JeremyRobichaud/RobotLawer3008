[1]            Seymour Lloyd Sharpe (Sharpe) was convicted by a jury of two counts of second degree murder.  The automatic penalty following such a conviction is one of life imprisonment.  The issue for determination is the parole ineligibility period.  The Crown position is that consecutive sentences are warranted.  They ask for no parole for 20 years for the murder of Natasha Jeffrey (Jeffrey) and 15 years consecutive for the murder of Ronald Dabreo (Dabreo), reduced to 30 years for the principle of totality.

[2]            The position of the defence is that the minimum 10-year ineligibility periods concurrent on both convictions would be appropriate in this case.

[3]            Pursuant to s. 745 of the Criminal Code, the range for each count of second degree murder is between 10 and 25 years.  This section provides as follows:

745     … the sentence to be pronounced against a person who is to be sentenced to imprisonment for life shall be

 

(c)      in respect of a person who has been convicted of second degree murder, that the person be sentenced to imprisonment for life without eligibility for parole until the person has served at least ten years of the sentence or such greater number of years, not being more than twenty-five years, as has been substituted therefor pursuant to section 745.4;
