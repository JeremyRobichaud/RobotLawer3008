The Facts
[2]             (1) On August 1, 1984, the accused was charged with second degree murder.

(2) The accused applied to a judge of Her Majesty’s Court of Queen’s Bench for Saskatchewan (the Queen’s Bench) on August 29, 1984; the learned judge determined that the detention of the accused was necessary, and dismissed the application.

(3) The preliminary hearing was concluded on November 16, 1984, and the accused was committed to stand trial on a charge of manslaughter.

(4) On December 20, 1984, he applied to a judge of the Provincial Court for judicial interim release. The application was refused because of the Queen’s Bench decision of August 29, 1984. The learned presiding judge had doubts about his jurisdiction by reason of the uncertainty arising from the cases on this point.
